Difference Between Synecdoche And Metonymy

Aug 29, 2024 Synecdoche and Metonymy are two literary devices that are often confused with each other, but they have very different meanings and uses. Synecdoche is a figure of speech in which a part of something is used to refer to the whole, while Metonymy is a figure of speech in which one thing is referred to by something related to it. Understanding these two concepts can help readers more fully understand and appreciate the use of figurative language in literature.
