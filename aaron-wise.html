What is the Professional Career of Aaron Wise?

After spending two years at Oregon, Aaron Wise made the transition to the workforce in 2016. At the 2016 US Open, his first major competition, he made his professional debut. He fired rounds of 74-76, missing the cut. On the PGA Tour Canada in 2016, he achieved his first professional victory at the Syncrude Oil Country Championship. He received a Web.com Tour card for the 2017 season after finishing fourth on the Order of Merit at the end of the 2016 season. He defeated runner-up Beau Hossler by five strokes to win the Air Capital Classic on the Korn Ferry Tour in June 2017.

He received a PGA Tour card for the 2018 season thanks to his victory. After placing 18th on the Web.com regular-season money list, he was promoted. In May 2018, he achieved his first PGA Tour victory at the AT&T Byron Nelson. He beat the runner-up Marc Leishman by three strokes with a final-round 65 to finish at 261 (-23). In the Bridgestone Invitational at the world golf championships, he tied for sixth place. At The Northern Trust, when he made his FedExCup Playoffs debut, he placed T5. He came in 24th place overall in the FedExCup standings.

The 2018 PGA Tour Rooke of the Year trophy was then given to him. He had 21 starts in the 2019 season, one top-10 result, and 15 cuts. In the 2019 FedExCup rankings, he came in at position No. 114.
